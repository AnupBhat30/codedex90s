1. Introduction to the 90s Internet

The 1990s marked the beginning of the World Wide Web as we know it today. It was a time of exploration and innovation, where users began to connect to the internet from their homes.

During this decade, the internet transformed from a niche tool used by researchers to a mainstream platform for communication, information, and entertainment.

2. Dial-Up Connections

Dial-up connections were the primary means of accessing the internet in the 90s. Users would connect their computers to a phone line using a modem, which made a series of beeping sounds as it established a connection.

While slow by today’s standards, dial-up was revolutionary at the time, allowing users to browse websites, send emails, and chat online.

3. Popular Browsers of the Era

Several web browsers emerged during the 90s, each contributing to the growth of the internet:

  • Netscape Navigator: One of the first popular web browsers, known for its user-friendly interface.
  • Internet Explorer: Released by Microsoft, it quickly became the dominant browser in the late 90s.
  • Opera: A lesser-known browser that introduced features like tabbed browsing.

4. Basic HTML and Web Design

HTML (HyperText Markup Language) was the backbone of web design in the 90s. It allowed users to create simple web pages with text, images, and links.

Learning basic HTML was essential for anyone looking to build a presence on the web. Simple tags like <h1> for headings and <a> for links were commonly used.

5. Online Communities and Culture

The 90s saw the rise of online communities, where users could connect with others who shared similar interests. Forums, chat rooms, and early social media platforms like AOL Instant Messenger became popular.

These communities fostered a sense of belonging and allowed users to share ideas, experiences, and creativity.

6. Safety and Etiquette

As the internet grew, so did the importance of online safety and etiquette. Users were encouraged to protect their personal information and be respectful in online interactions.

Understanding the unwritten rules of online communication was crucial for navigating the early web.
